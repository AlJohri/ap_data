Barbara Lee was born in El Paso, Texas, and now lives in Oakland, Calif. She earned a bachelor's degree from Mills College in 1973 and a master's degree in social welfare from the University of California-Berkeley in 1975.

Lee was the chief of staff for then-Rep. Ron Dellums from 1975 to 1987. She served in the California Assembly from 1990 to 1996 and in the California Senate from 1996 to 1998.

She was elected to the U.S. House in 1998, filling Dellum's seat when he resigned.

Lee is single and has two children.

Profile

Barbara Lee is a former social worker who has been an anti-war activist — regardless of which political party is in power.

Lee, who has represented California's 9th Congressional District since her election in 1998, is running for election in the newly drawn 13th District against political novice Marilyn Singleton.

Lee offered an amendment in July 2010 on a House measure to fund President Barack Obama's Afghanistan troop surge. She sought to end the war by limiting the new funding to the withdrawal of troops but her amendment failed.

In 2011, she voted to withdraw troops from Afghanistan. That same year, Lee also voted in favor of limiting funds appropriated for military operations in Libya.

In 2012, after the Supreme Court upheld the 2010 health care reform bill, Lee praised the decision calling it a "victory for the American people."

Lee was elected chairwoman in 2008 of the Congressional Black Caucus, which successfully urged that Roland Burris be seated as Illinois' junior senator after his controversial appointment in 2009 by disgraced then-Gov. Rod Blagojevich.

In November 2008, Lee helped House Speaker Nancy Pelosi rename a San Francisco job-training center on an old military base in honor of Harvey Milk, the slain gay activist and San Francisco city supervisor. It was believed to be the first federal building named for an openly gay person.

Lee criticized President Bill Clinton's bombing of Iraq in 1998, and she was the only member of Congress to vote against a 1999 resolution regarding Clinton's commitment of troops to Kosovo.

After Sept. 11, she cast the sole vote against a resolution approving the use of force against terrorists. She received threats and needed round-the-clock security. She co-founded the Out of Iraq Caucus and has opposed war-spending bills crafted by both parties.

She has proposed cutting the nation's defense budget and weapons stockpiles, and she lobbied to end the embargo against Cuba.

She was arrested in 2006 for protesting outside Sudan's embassy in Washington, D.C. Her Darfur divestment bill was signed into law in 2007.

Lee joined seven other House members in 2008 in proposing an end to federal prosecution for possessing up to 100 grams of marijuana — about 3.5 ounces — or for giving away up to an ounce of marijuana to someone else.

Lee co-founded the Out of Poverty Caucus and she undertook what she called a food stamp challenge in 2007, living for a week on $21 in groceries. She reported eating grits and toast for breakfast, bananas and crackers for lunch, and stretching a package of chicken thighs over several dinners.

She has introduced successful bills to create a global AIDS fund, protect AIDS orphans and spend $15 billion to fight AIDS, malaria and tuberculosis.

Lee is one of the leading congressional opponents of a 20-year-old ban on federal funding for needle-exchange programs. She and other opponents, including the NAACP and the National Urban League, say repealing the ban would help slow the HIV/AIDS epidemic among blacks.

In 2011, she introduced a bill that would provide states with incentives and support to reform criminal laws aimed at people with HIV.

She is one of 20 vice-chairs of the LGBT Equality Caucus.
