Jeff Denham was born in Hawthorne, Calif., and lives in Turlock. He earned a bachelor's in political science from California Polytechnic State University-San Luis Obispo.

Denham has worked for many years in farming as both an almond rancher and in starting a plastics container company to ship produce. He served on active and reserve status in the U.S. Air Force for 16 years, and served in both Operation Desert Storm in Iraq and Operation Restore Hope in Somalia.

He was a California state senator from 2002 to 2010, when he was elected to the U.S. House.

Denham and his wife, Sonia, have two children.

Profile

Jeff Denham, a conservative Republican state senator known for his long military career, won the open seat in California's traditionally Republican 19th Congressional District in the 2010 midterm election, helping the GOP seize control of the House of Representatives.

Denham, who enjoyed strong tea party during the election, filled the seat left open by Rep. George Radanovich, who decided not to seek another term. After Denham won a contentious 2010 Republican primary, he said that voters were most concerned about the national debt and in bringing more water to the region.

"Locally, our economy is upside down," Denham said in a 2010 interview. "We have one of the highest unemployment rates in the country due to our water crisis. We need a long-term solution to solving our water problems."

Denham had already demonstrated his political resilience in 2008 in the state Assembly, when he survived a recall attempt Democrats promoted after a budget fight.

But now that the boundaries of his district have changed, Denham faces a challenge from Democrat and former astronaut Jose Hernandez, as Denham seeks in 2012 to represent California's newly drawn 10th Congressional District.

Democrats are eyeing the district and throwing heavy financial support behind Hernandez, who they believe will appeal to the area's growing Hispanic population.

But they will go up against a veteran who served for 16 years on active and reserve status in the U.S. Air Force. Denham received meritorious service medals for his service in Operation Desert Storm (Iraq) and Operation Restore Hope (Somalia).

He has taken an active role on the House Veterans' Affairs Committee and introduced a bill to streamline the federal certification process to make it easier for veterans to use the skills they acquired in the military to find work when they return home.

Denham also sits on the Transportation and Infrastructure Committee and the Natural Resources Committee.

He has focused other legislative efforts on water issues, energy production and infrastructure development. Denham has been leading congressional efforts to investigate waste of taxpayer dollars by the General Services Administration. Another bill he sponsored would have cut back red tape for carrying out aspects of the Central Valley Project, a major issue for the large San Joaquin Valley farming interests he represents.

Denham also has sought an oversight hearing examining the viability of California's high-speed rail project after recent cost estimates more than doubled to $98.5 billion. The Republican used to support the proposal but later said that rising costs and construction-time projections have caused him to doubt its viability.
