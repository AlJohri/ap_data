Susan Brooks was born and raised in Fort Wayne, Ind., and now resides in Carmel. She earned a bachelor's from Miami University and a law degree from Indiana University-Indianapolis.

Brooks served as deputy mayor of Indianapolis under Mayor Steve Goldsmith from 1998 to 1999. She then practiced law for two years at Ice Miller in Indianapolis.

Beginning in 2001, Brooks served six years as U.S. Attorney for the Southern District of Indiana. She then became senior vice president and general counsel for Ivy Tech Community College.

She and her husband, David, have two children.

Profile

Susan Brooks is a newcomer to national political campaigns. She narrowly won Indiana's May 2012 Republican primary, defeating former U.S. Rep. David McIntosh by just 993 votes. She took 30 percent of the vote in an eight-person field.

Brooks is seeking to succeed retiring Republican Rep. Dan Burton, who never received less than 62 percent of the vote in all his general election victories.

Though Burton had little trouble winning general elections, he appeared vulnerable in the previous two primaries and Brooks announced she was running before Burton announced he was retiring. During the primary, New Jersey Gov. Chris Christie endorsed Brooks and attended a fundraiser for her.

If elected, Brooks would become the first Republican female member of Congress from Indiana since Cecil Murray Harden left office in 1959. Indiana has had only five female members of Congress from either party, none of them currently holding office.

Brooks says she wants to restore faith in Congress. She says a bill recently signed into law, which explicitly bans the president, members of Congress and thousands of other federal workers from profiting from nonpublic information learned on the job, should be strengthened.

She wants to slow the practice of former members of Congress becoming lobbyists. Rules now state that members of the House must wait one year after leaving Congress to become lobbyists, while senators must wait two years. Brooks supports extending the wait periods to six years, and says Congress should consider term limits.

Brooks believes Congress should not pass legislation that includes mandates on American citizens that don't apply to members of Congress, and she wants to prevent corrupt politicians from receiving federal pensions.

She says Congress should cut taxes to help make American businesses more competitive globally. She says the proper role of government in job creation is to create an environment of certainty. She also says Congress must cut wasteful spending, simplify the tax code and allow fewer loopholes.

Brooks wants to repeal the 2010 health care reform bill, saying it will cost too much and lead to job losses. She says it should be replaced with free-market policies that allow individuals to decide where their health care dollars are spent.
