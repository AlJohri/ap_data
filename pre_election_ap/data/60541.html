Mike Kelly was born in Pittsburgh and resides in Butler. He played football in high school and college and earned a bachelor's degree from Notre Dame University in 1970.

He owns Kelly Chevrolet-Cadillac Inc., a business started by his father in 1953, which he purchased in 1995. He also owns Mike Kelly Hyundai and Mike Kelly Automotive. Kelly has been active for years in local government and charitable work through the auto dealerships.

Kelly served as a board member of the Housing and Redevelopment Authority of Butler County, and as a Butler city councilman.

He was elected to the U.S. House in 2010.

Kelly and his wife, Victoria, have four children.

Profile

Mike Kelly unseated freshman Democratic Rep. Kathy Dahlkemper in 2010 to win election to Pennsylvania's 3rd Congressional District seat. It was a victory that came after he bested five other candidates in the Republican primary that year.

In his bid for a second term, he faces Democrat Missy Eaton in the November 2012 election.

Kelly says he is a fiscal conservative who believes lower taxes and less regulation are the best way to foster small business, create economic growth and stoke new hiring.

Kelly opposes higher federal taxes and spending. He ran on his opposition to the 2009 approximately $800 billion economic stimulus package and the landmark 2010 health care reform bill, both backed by President Barack Obama.

He follows the Republican Party line of preferring to limit civil liability exposure for medical malpractice and allowing health insurers to compete across state lines.

Kelly also opposes any further gun-control laws.

In 2011, the Rev. Al Sharpton and liberal groups criticized Kelly over his holdings in two gas and oil companies. Opponents claimed the holdings amounted to a conflict of interest because he has supported continuing federal subsidies for the oil industry. While Kelly sold his interests, he defended his stake in the companies, which were both founded by his wife's family.
