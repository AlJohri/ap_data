Jose Serrano was born in Mayaguez, Puerto Rico, and now resides in the Bronx, N.Y. Serrano and his family moved to New York from Puerto Rico in 1950. He completed courses at the American Institute of Banking and at the City University of New York's Lehman College.

Serrano served in the Army Medical Corps from 1964 to 1966. He was chairman of the South Bronx Community Corporation and an employee of the community school board from 1969 to 1974.

Serrano was elected to the New York Assembly in 1974 and served until 1990, when he won a special election to the U.S. House to replace Rep. Robert Garcia, who resigned after his conviction on extortion and conspiracy charges stemming from the Wedtech military contracting scandal.

Serrano is divorced and has five children.

Profile

Jose Serrano, the representative from New York's 16th Congressional District, has never faced a serious challenge in his heavily Democratic district and it appears he won't in 2012, either.

His opponent, Republican Frank Della Valle, has lost badly to Serrano twice before, never gaining more than 8 percent of the vote.

Due to redistricting following the 2010 Census, Serrano will be running in the 15th District in 2012.

Serrano's initiatives have focused on immigration reform and the environment, and he has earned a 100 percent rating for his environmental record from various advocacy groups. His efforts to help clean up the Bronx River were rewarded when a beaver — the first seen in New York City in 200 years — was found on the river in 2007. The beaver, Jose, was named for the congressman.

In June 2012, when the U.S. Supreme Court struck down parts of Arizona's tough and controversial immigration law, Serrano called it a partial victory but criticized the court for leaving in place a provision allowing police to check the immigration status of people stopped for other reasons.

"It is my belief that this section of the law will lead to rampant racial profiling, and that it is clearly unconstitutional," he said in June 2012.

Serrano introduced a bill in 2009 that would give judges discretion in deportation proceedings. He wrote to the commissioner of Major League Baseball in 2010 requesting that the 2011 All-Star game be moved out of Arizona because of that state's 2010 immigration law.

He pushed to get New York out of the federal "Secure Communities" program that was criticized by immigrant advocates.

Serrano was the only New York City lawmaker to vote against the 2008 $700 billion bailout of the financial industry. He said the bill favored the wealthy and ignored the poor. Serrano did vote for the 2009 approximately $800 billion economic stimulus package and the 2010 health care reform bill.

Like many New York City Democrats, Serrano has argued consistently against the "misguided war" in Iraq, joining five other members of Congress who sued in federal court.

Before he came to Washington, Serrano served in the New York state Assembly, where he worked to save social services threatened by fiscal problems. He worked principally on consumer affairs issues and education.

In 1987, the City Council delegation from the Bronx backed down from a tacit understanding that they would appoint Serrano borough president after he declared the Bronx had become "a fetid sinkhole of corruption."

Serrano was the first native Puerto Rican to become a voting member of Congress. He is Congress' most senior member of Puerto Rican descent and continues to work for equal treatment of the territory.
