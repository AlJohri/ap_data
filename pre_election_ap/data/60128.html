Ted Deutch was born and raised in Bethlehem, Penn., and now lives in West Boca Raton, Fla. He earned bachelor's and law degrees from the University of Michigan.

Deutch worked as an attorney before winning election to the Florida Senate in 2006.

He won a special election to the U.S. House in April 2010.

Deutch and his wife, Jill, have three children.

Profile

Ted Deutch was among a group of congressmen who, in 2012, sought the toughest possible sanctions against Iran over the country's suspected nuclear weapons program.

As Congress debated sanctions, Deutch pushed negotiators to blacklist Iran's energy sector, labeling it a "zone of proliferation concern" in June 2012. That would effectively ban all business with Iran.

He also co-introduced a bill in April 2012 called the Palestinian Peace Promotion and Anti-Incitement Act that would prohibit U.S. financial assistance to the Palestinian Authority unless the president determined assistance was important to national security.

Deutch won a special election to Florida's 19th Congressional District seat in April 2010, replacing Democratic Rep. Robert Wexler, who left to head a Middle East think tank. It was the first U.S. House race of 2010.

Like many 2010 races, the election pitted a candidate critical of President Barack Obama's administration — Republican Ed Lynch — against a Democrat defending the 2010 health care reform bill, the 2009 economic stimulus package and other measures passed the two previous years.

Deutch said his victory was a sign Democrats still have strong support in Broward and Palm Beach counties.

Deutch served the remaining eight months of Wexler's term before winning re-election in November 2010 against Republican Joe Budd.

In his first months in office, Deutch supported the 2010 financial regulation reform bill and the $26 billion state-aid legislation expected to save hundreds of thousands of education and public sector jobs.

Deutch previously was a state senator, representing a district that included Palm Beach County and north Broward County. He sponsored legislation later passed into law making Florida the first state to outlaw pension fund investments in any company doing business with Iran's energy sector or Sudan.

Deutch also was successful in pushing a bill that increased Florida's tax on cigarettes by $1 per pack.

In 2012, following redistricting due to the results of the 2010 census, Deutch is running for Florida's 21st Congressional District.
